What Are eBooks?

[ News Online ] [ Class Material Online ] [ Paperless Office ]

eBooks, or electronic books, are digital versions of traditional printed books that can be read on devices like smartphones, tablets, e-readers, and computers. Instead of turning physical pages, readers swipe or tap to move through chapters. Many popular titles—novels, biographies, self-help books, and textbooks—are now available in both print and digital formats.

For readers who only pick up a few books a year, eBooks offer a convenient way to read without needing to visit a bookstore or library. A book can be downloaded in seconds and read almost anywhere, whether at home, on a commute, or while traveling.

Why People Choose eBooks

One of the biggest advantages of eBooks is convenience. Readers can carry an entire library on a single device, making it easy to switch between books or revisit old favorites. Features like adjustable font size, built-in dictionaries, and night-reading modes can also make reading more comfortable, especially for those with vision strain or limited lighting.

Cost can be another factor. eBooks are often cheaper than hardcover editions, and many classics or promotional titles are available for free. For casual readers, this lowers the barrier to trying new authors or genres without a large commitment.

How eBooks Fit Into Everyday Reading

For people who read a few books a year, eBooks don’t necessarily replace physical books—they often complement them. Some readers enjoy print books at home but prefer eBooks when traveling or waiting in line. Others appreciate being able to sample a chapter digitally before deciding whether to purchase a physical copy.

Libraries and bookstores also support eBooks, allowing users to borrow or buy titles online. This flexibility makes reading more accessible, even for those with busy schedules or limited storage space at home.

Are eBooks Right for You?

Whether eBooks are a good fit depends on personal preference. Some readers still prefer the feel and experience of a printed book, while others value the portability and features of digital reading. For many adults, eBooks provide an easy, low-effort way to read more often—without changing how they already enjoy books. The most popular device for reading E-Books is the Kindle.
